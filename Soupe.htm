Soupe à l’oignon

Soupe à l’oignon, a classic French dish, is a hearty soup made primarily of onions that are gently fried and then cooked in meat stock or water. The dish is traditionally served gratinéed, meaning it’s topped with croutons or a larger piece of bread covered with cheese floating on top. Originating in France, this soup has been enjoyed since medieval times, and the modern version dates back to the mid-19th century. It’s known for its comforting and warming qualities.To prepare this amazing dish, here are some Ingredients and tips.

Ingredients

 

  • 6 large onions
  • 8 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 4 cups of bread crumbs
  • 4 cups of Gruyère cheese
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 ovenproof bowls

 

Here are some steps to prepare     Soupe à l’oignon:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and set it to broil.
  2. Peel and slice the onions into thin rounds. Place them in a large bowl and pour the chicken or vegetable broth over them. Let them sit for about 30 minutes.
  3. While the onions are sitting, you can start preparing the bread crumbs. Mince the garlic and mix it with the breadcrumbs, along with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  4. Once the onions have absorbed enough of the broth, transfer them to the oven and broil them until they are nicely caramelized and the edges are golden brown. This should take about 15-20 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, in a separate bowl, mix the Gruyère cheese with a little more chicken or vegetable broth until the cheese is fully melted.
  6. Pour the melted cheese mixture into the ovenproof bowls. Place a spoonful of the caramelized onions on top of each portion of cheese.
  7. Sprinkle the bread crumb mixture on top of the onions, then cover with more caramelized onions.
  8. Return the bowls to the oven and broil them until the cheese is melted and slightly browned. This should take about 5-10 minutes.
  9. Remove the bowls from the oven and serve immediately. Each bowl should be a mix of melted cheese, broth, tender onions, and bread.

If you want to make the best Soupe à l’oignon, here are some tips:

  • Choose the Right Onions: Choose large, firm onions for your soup. Their size will affect the flavor and texture of the soup.
  • Caramelize the Onions: Caramelizing the onions is a key step in making Soupe à l’oignon. This means slowly cooking the onions in a bit of butter until they are golden brown and slightly sweet.
  • Balance Your Flavors: The key to a good Soupe à l’oignon is balance. The onions should be sweet and savory, complemented by the acidity of the wine. The cheese should also be added towards the end of cooking to create a nice contrast of flavors.
  • Don’t Skimp on the Butter: A generous amount of butter is needed to properly caramelize the onions and infuse the soup with flavor.
  • Serve Hot: Serving the soup hot will enhance the flavors and ensure the cheese melts beautifully
